# Communications Playbook

## Purpose

This playbook helps contributors talk about Accountability Atlas publicly in a way that is credible, lawful, and safe.

---

## Core message

Accountability Atlas is an open-source civic-tech project for verified evidence, legal/humanitarian tracking, and lawful citizen action in support of civilian protection and atrocity accountability.

---

## One-line pitch

Verified evidence. Lawful pressure. Civilian protection.

---

## Short pitch

Accountability Atlas helps citizens, journalists, researchers, and policymakers navigate verified public evidence, legal developments, humanitarian information, and lawful action pathways around atrocity accountability.

---

## Founder/contributor pitch

We are building a calm, credible civic-tech platform that turns verified public evidence into practical, lawful pressure for civilian protection, humanitarian access, and legal accountability.

---

## What to emphasize

- evidence and source methodology;
- lawful action;
- humanitarian access;
- civilian protection;
- legal accountability;
- anti-hate and anti-doxing rules;
- open-source collaboration;
- no false partnership claims;
- do-no-harm principles.

---

## What to avoid

Avoid language that sounds like:

- revenge;
- harassment;
- collective blame;
- propaganda;
- guilt before judgment;
- incitement;
- doxing;
- spam campaigns;
- “we speak for victims.”

---

## FAQ

### Is this a campaign against a people?

No. The project opposes harm to civilians, atrocity crimes, humanitarian obstruction, and impunity. It does not target identity groups or civilians.

### Is this a legal authority?

No. The platform organizes public records, legal developments, NGO conclusions, and evidence sources. Courts and competent legal bodies make legal determinations.

### Does the platform accept witness evidence?

Not yet. Sensitive submissions require expert-reviewed security, legal, privacy, and human-rights workflows.

### Are listed organizations partners?

Not unless clearly marked as verified partners. Many entries are public resource listings only.

### Does the platform handle donations?

Not in the MVP. It may link to official organization donation pages.

## Crisis communication

If the project is attacked publicly:

1. do not respond emotionally;
2. gather facts;
3. consult maintainers;
4. correct any real issue;
5. publish a calm statement if needed;
6. avoid quote-tweet fights or pile-ons;
7. do not expose private communications.

---
