# Anti-Doxing and Targeting Policy

## Purpose

This project must never become a tool for harassment, intimidation, or private-person targeting.

---

## Definition

Doxing is the collection, publication, or amplification of private or identifying information in a way that could expose a person to harassment, intimidation, physical danger, reputational harm, or unwanted contact.

---

## Prohibited information

Do not publish or submit:

- private addresses;
- personal phone numbers;
- private email addresses;
- family member details;
- school addresses;
- workplace details of private persons;
- private social media profiles;
- identity documents;
- license plates unless already part of official public evidence and necessary;
- facial images of private persons for targeting;
- revenge lists;
- “find this person” requests;
- instructions to contact, shame, harass, or expose private persons.

---

## Public figures and officials

Public officials, government representatives, corporate actors, and named people in legal proceedings may be discussed only using publicly relevant information.

Allowed:

- official role;
- public statement;
- public vote;
- official contact channel;
- court record;
- sanctions listing;
- official biography;
- public institutional email or office contact.

Not allowed:

- home address;
- private phone;
- family details;
- non-public travel plans;
- personal harassment instructions.

---

## Military personnel and combatants

Do not publish private identifying information about individual soldiers or combatants unless:

- it appears in an official public legal record;
- it is necessary for a legally relevant accountability entry;
- it has been reviewed for safety and legality;
- it does not include private contact/location details.

The platform should focus on institutions, command responsibility, public officials, official units where legally relevant, and court-documented allegations.

---

## Social media

Do not use the repository to collect lists of private social media accounts for targeting.

Do not encourage followers to contact, shame, or harass people through social platforms.

---

## Action hub limits

Action tools may target:

- elected representatives;
- public institutions;
- ministries;
- official offices;
- international bodies;
- corporate public accountability channels where relevant.

Action tools may not target:

- private individuals;
- families;
- private workplaces;
- personal social accounts;
- schools;
- religious communities.

---

## Takedown process

If doxing material is discovered:

1. remove it immediately if possible;
2. preserve private audit note for maintainers;
3. review how it entered the project;
4. notify affected maintainers/reviewers if necessary;
5. update filters or guidance;
6. document the decision if appropriate without republishing the data.

---

## Contributor warning

Any contributor who intentionally adds doxing material may be removed from the project.
