# Mission and Positioning

## Mission

Accountability Atlas exists to organize verified public evidence, legal developments, humanitarian information, and lawful citizen actions in support of civilian protection, humanitarian access, and accountability for alleged atrocity crimes.

---

## Core promise

**Verified evidence. Lawful pressure. Civilian protection.**

The platform should help people move from outrage to informed action.

---

## What the platform is

Accountability Atlas is:

- a civic-technology project;
- a public evidence and action hub;
- a policy-pressure tool;
- a legal and humanitarian tracker;
- a source-methodology project;
- a coalition and organization directory;
- a future framework for multiple atrocity contexts.

---

## What the platform is not

Accountability Atlas is not:

- a revenge platform;
- a doxing project;
- a hate campaign;
- a replacement for courts;
- a substitute for humanitarian organizations;
- a secure witness intake system yet;
- a donation intermediary at launch;
- a platform for harassment or incitement.

---

## Primary audiences

### Public citizens

People who want to understand what is happening and take lawful action.

### Journalists

People who need source-linked, organized, credible context.

### Policymakers and staffers

People who need briefings, country pages, action asks, and evidence summaries.

### NGOs and humanitarian organizations

Organizations whose public work should be discoverable and correctly represented.

### Lawyers, researchers, and OSINT contributors

People who can help improve source methodology and evidence standards.

### Volunteers and contributors

People who want to help build public-interest infrastructure.

---

## Strategic positioning

The platform should sound like:

- a civic institution;
- a human-rights documentation project;
- a policy briefing tool;
- a serious open-source public-interest project.

It should not sound like:

- a rage account;
- a partisan meme page;
- a revenge campaign;
- a conspiracy archive;
- a harassment network.

---

## Approved positioning statement

Accountability Atlas is an open-source civic-tech project that organizes verified public evidence, legal developments, humanitarian information, and lawful action pathways for civilian protection and atrocity accountability.

---

## Short public description

A calm, credible platform for verified evidence, legal tracking, humanitarian accountability, and lawful citizen action.

---

## One-sentence contributor pitch

We are building open-source civic infrastructure that helps people turn verified evidence into lawful pressure for civilian protection and accountability.
