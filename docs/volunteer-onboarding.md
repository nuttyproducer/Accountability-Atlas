# Volunteer Onboarding

Welcome to Accountability Atlas.

This project is serious, safety-sensitive, and built by volunteers. The best contribution is not always the biggest one. Small, careful work matters.

---

## Step 1 — Understand the mission

Read:

- `README.md`
- `docs/mission-and-positioning.md`
- `docs/ethics-and-safety.md`

You should understand that this project is about verified evidence, lawful pressure, humanitarian accountability, and civilian protection.

---

## Step 2 — Choose your contribution lane

Possible lanes:

- frontend development;
- backend/data architecture;
- design and accessibility;
- research and source review;
- legal language review;
- security and privacy review;
- writing and communications;
- translation;
- project coordination;
- issue triage.

## Step 4 — Pick a small task

Look for labels:

- `good first issue`
- `documentation`
- `design`
- `research`
- `frontend`
- `translation`
- `help wanted`

Comment before starting.

---

## Step 5 — Work safely

Do not add:

- private personal data;
- raw sensitive media;
- witness testimony;
- graphic content;
- unverified allegations;
- private outreach contacts;
- API keys or secrets.

---

## Step 6 — Open a pull request

Use the pull request template.

Keep the PR small and focused.

---

## Time expectations

The best early contribution pattern is:

- 2–4 hours per week;
- 4-week commitment;
- one small deliverable;
- clear handoff if unavailable.

---

## Contributor wellbeing

This subject can be emotionally heavy. Please do not overload yourself.

Healthy contribution is better than burnout.
