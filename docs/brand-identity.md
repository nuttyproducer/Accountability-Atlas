My honest view: the brand should **not** look like a protest poster, rage campaign, war-news website, or charity guilt page. It should look like a **credible civic institution** that combines:

**human rights + legal accountability + evidence archive + citizen action + humanitarian protection.**

Think:

> Amnesty-level moral clarityBellingcat-level evidence disciplineUN-report calmnesscivic-tech usabilityhuman warmth, but no chaos

The brand problem we need to solve
----------------------------------

This platform deals with extreme human suffering. So the brand needs to hold two things at once:

**Urgency**People must feel: “This matters now.”

**Credibility**Decision-makers, lawyers, journalists, NGOs, and contributors must feel: “This is serious, sourced, and safe.”

So we avoid:

`blood-red rage branding  military aesthetics  aggressive anti-state slogans  shocking gore visuals  anonymous hacker vibes  conspiracy-style design  overly emotional typography`

We aim for:

`calm authority  human dignity  evidence-first structure  quiet pressure  institutional trust  global civic participation`

Best brand direction
--------------------

For **Accountability Atlas**, I would choose:

Brand Direction: “The Calm Pressure System”
===========================================

The platform should feel like a global evidence room. Not cold. Not academic to the point of distance. But calm, weighty, and impossible to dismiss.

The core feeling:

> “Here is the evidence. Here is the legal context. Here is who has power. Here is what citizens can do.”

That is the brand.

Brand personality
-----------------

I would define the personality like this:

`Serious but not cold  Human but not sentimental  Urgent but not chaotic  Moral but not hateful  Transparent but not reckless  Global but grounded  Technical but readable`

This is important because the tone will affect everything: homepage, buttons, colors, issue labels, contributor docs, social media, outreach emails, and future reports.

Name check: Accountability Atlas
--------------------------------

I still think **Accountability Atlas** is very strong.

Why it works:

*   **Accountability** = legal, political, moral responsibility
    
*   **Atlas** = global map, structured knowledge, navigation
    
*   It does not sound like a rage campaign

*   It works for policy, data, action, and documentation

Possible tagline:

`Evidence for protection. Pressure for accountability.`

Or calmer:

`Mapping evidence. Mobilizing lawful action.`

Or more institutional:

`A civic platform for atrocity documentation, humanitarian accountability, and lawful public action.`

My favorite:

`Evidence for protection. Action for accountability.`

Visual identity concept
-----------------------

The logo should not be a fist, weapon, flag, crying child, blood drop, or destroyed building.

Better symbols:

`map grid  atlas lines  coordinate pin  document seal  witness light  circle of protection  compass  archive box  signal lines`

A strong logo concept:

**A globe/grid mark with one illuminated coordinate point, surrounded by a subtle protective circle.**

Meaning:

*   the world is watching
    
*   evidence is located
    
*   civilians are protected
    
*   accountability is global
    
*   no aggressive symbolism
    

Another concept:

**A folded map + document page hybrid.**

Meaning:

*   geography + evidence
    
*   map + legal dossier
    
*   archive + action

Typography
----------

Use open-source fonts. Keep it accessible and professional.

My recommendation:

`Headings: IBM Plex Serif or Source Serif 4  Body: Inter or IBM Plex Sans  Data/UI labels: IBM Plex Mono`

Why:

*   serif headings add legal/editorial weight
    
*   sans body keeps it readable
    
*   mono labels work well for evidence IDs, timestamps, coordinates, source references
    

Example feel:

`H1: IBM Plex Serif, strong, calm  Body: Inter, highly readable  Metadata: IBM Plex Mono, small labels`

UI style
--------

The interface should feel like a **public evidence dashboard**, not a SaaS marketing product.

Design language:

`cards  source badges  verification levels  timeline strips  map grid patterns  document previews  country scorecards  subtle dividers  clear metadata  downloadable dossier blocks`

Avoid too many animations. Use subtle movement only:

`slow map-grid fade  document card reveal  soft counter updates  hover states  timeline progress`

No flashy parallax war drama. This project needs trust.

Three possible brand moods
--------------------------

### Option A — Institutional Archive

Most serious. Best for EU/UN/legal audiences.

Feels like:

`policy institute  legal archive  human-rights observatory`

Visuals:

`paper textures  document cards  map grids  formal typography  dark navy  warm white`

Best if you want maximum credibility.

### Option B — Civic Action Platform

More accessible. Best for public contributors and citizens.

Feels like:

`modern NGO  civic tech  public participation`

Visuals:

`clean cards  clear CTAs  country pages  soft illustrations  action counters`

Best if you want people to actually use it.

### Option C — Witness & Memory Platform

More emotional and human.

Feels like:

`memorial archive  testimony vault  human stories`

Visuals:

`soft light  portrait-safe layouts  minimal color  quiet typography`

Powerful, but risky if too emotional too early.

My recommendation
-----------------

Combine **A + B** first.

So:

`70% Institutional Archive  30% Civic Action Platform  0% Rage campaign  0% shock content`

Later, when the testimony vault exists safely, add a small amount of **Witness & Memory** feeling.

Brand principles
----------------

These should guide every design and text decision:

1. Accuracy before speed.  
2. Protection before publication.  
3. Evidence before opinion.  
4. Accountability without dehumanization.  
5. Lawful action only.  
6. Human dignity always.  
7. Transparency where safe.  
8. Privacy where necessary.

That is basically your brand constitution.

Design advice
-----------------------------

Do not over-design this.

The first version should be visually clean, almost boring in a good way. The trust comes from clarity, not decoration.

Your best landing page will probably be:

`warm background  strong typography  one powerful line  clear explanation  3–4 feature cards  methodology preview  contributor CTA  no drama  no gore  no overloaded animations`

That is how you make people take it seriously.
