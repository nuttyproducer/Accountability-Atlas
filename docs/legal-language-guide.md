# Legal Language Guide

**Status:** Active — all contributors writing about legal proceedings must follow this guide.  
**Last reviewed:** 2026-07-12  
**Companion to:** `docs/legal-language-policy.md` (principles), `docs/legal-wording-review-checklist.md` (review checklist)

---

## Purpose

This guide defines approved and prohibited language patterns for legal
content on Accountability Atlas. It is more specific than the legal-language
policy and is the primary reference for contributors drafting or reviewing
legal text.

---

## 1. Genocide terminology

### Approved patterns

| Context | Approved language | Example |
|---|---|---|
| ICJ proceedings — provisional measures stage | "The ICJ issued provisional measures in the case concerning alleged violations of the Genocide Convention." | In full context, with date and case name |
| ICJ proceedings — merits pending | "The Court has not issued a final judgment on the merits of the genocide allegation." | Always stated when describing ICJ proceedings |
| NGO determination | "Amnesty International concluded in its [date] report that [conduct] amounts to genocide, according to its published legal analysis." | Attributed to specific report and date |
| UN finding | "The UN Commission of Inquiry found reasonable grounds to believe that [conduct] may constitute acts of genocide." | Attributed to specific COI report |
| Expert legal determination | "[Named expert or body] determined that [conduct] meets the definition of genocide under Article II of the Genocide Convention." | Attributed to named expert or body |
| Platform's own position | "The platform supports lawful action to prevent and punish atrocity crimes, including alleged genocide, war crimes, and crimes against humanity." | Clearly labelled as the platform's advocacy position |

### Prohibited patterns

| Pattern | Why prohibited | Correction |
|---|---|---|
| "The genocide in Gaza" (unattributed) | Implies a final determination where none exists | Attribute to specific proceeding or finding |
| "Israel is committing genocide" (unattributed editorial) | States legal guilt without attribution | "South Africa alleges that Israel is violating the Genocide Convention" or attribute to specific finding |
| "Genocide has been proven" | No court has issued a final judgment of genocide | "The ICJ found a plausible basis for rights under the Genocide Convention" (provisional measures standard) |
| "The court proved genocide" | Courts issue judgments, not "proof" | "The court found that…" |
| "It is established that genocide is occurring" | Passive voice obscures who established it | Attribute to the specific body that made the finding |

## 2. Alleged crimes

### Approved patterns

- "alleged war crimes" / "alleged crimes against humanity" / "alleged violations of IHL"
- "[Prosecutor] alleges that [person] bears criminal responsibility for [charges]"
- "[Organisation] reported evidence consistent with possible war crimes"
- "allegations under investigation by [body]"

### Prohibited patterns

- "war crimes were committed" (unattributed)
- "proven war criminal" (no conviction exists)
- "[Person] is guilty of crimes against humanity" (no final judgment)

---

## 3. Provisional measures

### Approved patterns

- "The ICJ issued provisional measures on [date]"
- "Provisional measures are binding under international law"
- "Provisional measures do not constitute a final determination on the merits"
- "The Court ordered [state] to [specific measure]"

### Prohibited patterns

- "The ICJ ruled that genocide is occurring" (provisional measures ≠ merits ruling)
- "The Court found [state] guilty" (the ICJ does not issue criminal guilt findings)
- Treating provisional measures as equivalent to a final judgment

---

## 4. Arrest warrants

### Approved patterns

- "The ICC Pre-Trial Chamber issued an arrest warrant for [name] on [date]"
- "The warrant alleges criminal responsibility for [charges]"
- "An arrest warrant is a judicial finding of reasonable grounds — it is not a conviction"
- "All persons are presumed innocent until proven guilty"

### Prohibited patterns

- "[Name] is a war criminal" (no conviction exists)
- "[Name] has been convicted by the ICC" (unless a final conviction exists)
- Using "arrest warrant" and "conviction" interchangeably

---

## 5. Investigations

### Approved patterns

- "The ICC Office of the Prosecutor opened an investigation into [situation] on [date]"
- "The investigation is ongoing"
- "The prosecutor has not yet [filed charges / requested warrants / concluded]"
- "An investigation is not a finding of guilt"

### Prohibited patterns

- Implying that an investigation confirms the alleged crimes
- "The ICC is prosecuting [person]" before charges are confirmed
- "Under investigation for war crimes" (passive, unattributed — say who is investigating)

---

## 6. UN findings

### Approved patterns

- "The UN Commission of Inquiry found that…" (attributed to specific COI)
- "The UN Human Rights Council resolution [number] expressed concern about…"
- "UN OCHA reported that…" (for humanitarian updates, not legal findings)
- "The UN Secretary-General stated that…"

### Prohibited patterns

- "The UN found [state] guilty" (the UN does not issue criminal guilt findings)
- Treating a COI finding as equivalent to a court judgment
- "The UN confirmed" — use the specific body: "The UN Human Rights Committee concluded…"

---

## 7. NGO determinations

### Approved patterns

- "[NGO name] concluded in its [date] report that…"
- "[NGO name]'s published methodology describes…"
- "According to [NGO name]'s legal analysis…"
- "NGO determinations are distinct from judicial determinations"

### Prohibited patterns

- Presenting an NGO finding as a legal fact without attribution
- "Human rights organisations have confirmed" (vague — name the organisation)
- Implying that an NGO determination carries the same weight as a court judgment

---

## 8. Final judgments

### Approved patterns

- "The Court issued its final judgment on [date], finding that…"
- "The judgment is [appealable / final / subject to review]"
- "The Court ordered [remedy / reparation / specific action]"

### Prohibited patterns

- Describing a non-final ruling as a "final judgment"
- Implying that a judgment resolves all related legal questions
- "The case is closed" when appeals are possible or pending

---

## 9. Individual responsibility

### Approved patterns

- "Individual criminal responsibility under Article 25 of the Rome Statute"
- "Command responsibility where public legal sources support it"
- Using named individuals only when they appear in public legal proceedings
- "Public official [name], in their official capacity"

### Prohibited patterns

- Private addresses, family-member names, personal contact information
- Social-media harassment targets or revenge lists
- "They are responsible" (unattributed) — use "alleged to bear responsibility" or cite the legal source
- Collective-blame language targeting civilians or ethnic/religious/national groups

---

## 10. Institutional responsibility

### Approved patterns

- "[State] is a State Party to [treaty] and has obligations under Article [x]"
- "[Institution] has the legal competence to [action]"
- "[Institution] does not have the authority to [action] — this is a [member-state / national] competency"
- "The [body] found that [state] failed to comply with [obligation]"

### Prohibited patterns

- Implying that an institution has authority it does not possess
- "[State] is responsible for genocide" (unattributed)
- Generalising responsibility to a population: "The [nationality] people are responsible"
- Confusing EU institutional responsibility with member-state responsibility

---

## 11. Source attribution

### Approved patterns

- "According to [source], published [date], accessed [date]"
- "[Publisher] reported that…"
- "As documented in [document title] ([court/body], [date])"
- Linking to the primary source URL

### Prohibited patterns

- Citing this platform as the primary source
- "Sources say" (vague)
- Citing a news article when the primary source is available
- Using search-result snippets as source text without verifying the original

---

## 12. Contested claims

### Approved patterns

- "This claim is contested. [Party A] asserts [x], while [Party B] asserts [y]."
- "The factual basis for this claim is disputed by [party with standing]."
- "The [court/body] has not yet determined [specific question]."

### Prohibited patterns

- Presenting a contested claim as settled
- "Some say [x], others say [y]" (vague — name the parties)
- Implying that contestation means the claim is false

---

## 13. Right of reply

### Approved patterns

- "[Institution] responded to this characterisation on [date], stating that…"
- "This summary has been updated to reflect [institution]'s response."
- "The platform has offered [institution] an opportunity to respond."

### Prohibited patterns

- "[Institution] refused to comment" (unless they explicitly refused)
- Implying that lack of response confirms the platform's characterisation
- Publishing an institution's response selectively or out of context
