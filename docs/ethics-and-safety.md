# Ethics and Safety Policy

## Purpose

Accountability Atlas must help protect people, not create new risks. This document defines the project’s ethical boundaries.

---

## Core ethics

1. Do no harm.
2. Protect civilians.
3. Protect witnesses and vulnerable people.
4. Avoid hate and collective blame.
5. Avoid doxing and harassment.
6. Use lawful civic action only.
7. Preserve accuracy over virality.
8. Respect dignity in suffering.
9. Be transparent about uncertainty.
10. Correct mistakes quickly.

---

## Civilian protection standard

The platform opposes harm to civilians regardless of identity, nationality, ethnicity, religion, or political affiliation.

The platform does not justify attacks on civilians.

---

## Anti-hate standard

The platform does not tolerate:

- antisemitism;
- Islamophobia;
- anti-Arab racism;
- racism;
- xenophobia;
- ethnic hatred;
- dehumanization;
- collective blame.

Criticism of states, governments, militaries, policies, public officials, armed groups, and institutions must not become hatred toward identity groups or civilians.

---

## Anti-incitement standard

Do not use this project to encourage:

- violence;
- threats;
- harassment;
- vandalism;
- sabotage;
- stalking;
- doxing;
- intimidation;
- illegal activity.

---

## Trauma-informed content

Human suffering should not be used as engagement bait.

Avoid:

- graphic thumbnails;
- shock copy;
- repeated graphic media;
- sensational captions;
- exposing victims without consent;
- turning individual pain into shareable content without care.

When graphic content is necessary for documentation, use warnings, previews without gore, and strict access controls where appropriate.

---

## Witness safety

Until expert-reviewed systems exist, the project does not accept sensitive witness submissions.

Do not ask people in dangerous contexts to upload:

- raw video;
- exact location;
- identity documents;
- private testimony;
- contact details;
- evidence that could expose them.

Future witness workflows require:

- threat modeling;
- legal review;
- security review;
- trauma-informed design;
- data-retention policy;
- trained moderators;
- emergency takedown process.

---

## Public action safety

Action tools must be lawful and respectful.

Allowed:

- email representatives;
- call scripts;
- petitions where legal;
- public letters;
- policy brief sharing;
- peaceful protest calendar links;
- donation links to official organization pages.

Not allowed:

- spam campaigns;
- harassment campaigns;
- targeting private individuals;
- evading platform rules;
- threats;
- illegal disruption;
- doxing.

---

## Data minimization

Collect the least data possible.

For MVP:

- avoid user accounts;
- avoid tracking cookies;
- avoid unnecessary analytics;
- avoid storing IP addresses;
- avoid sensitive submissions;
- avoid private messaging features.

---

## Partner respect

Do not imply that an organization supports or partners with Accountability Atlas unless written confirmation exists.

Public organization directory entries must include a disclaimer if no partnership exists.

---

## Contributor wellbeing

This topic is heavy. Contributors should:

- take breaks;
- avoid graphic content where unnecessary;
- rotate moderation duties;
- avoid doomscrolling as work;
- use content warnings;
- ask for help;
- not carry the whole project alone.

---

## Safety review triggers

A safety review is required when adding:

- user submissions;
- maps with exact locations;
- media uploads;
- graphic content;
- named individuals;
- donation flows;
- live analytics;
- automated action tools;
- social media scraping;
- private collaboration channels.
