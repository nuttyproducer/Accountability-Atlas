# Partnership Policy

## Purpose

This policy prevents false claims of support, endorsement, or collaboration.

---

## Core rule

No organization may be described as a partner, verified partner, supporter, collaborator, advisor, sponsor, donor, or endorser unless written confirmation exists.

---

## Directory categories

### Public resource listed

Used when the platform lists publicly available information about an organization.

Required disclaimer:

> This directory entry uses public information. This organization has not necessarily reviewed, endorsed, or partnered with Accountability Atlas.

### Reviewed listing

Used when an organization has corrected or confirmed its public listing but has not agreed to partnership.

Required disclaimer:

> This organization reviewed its directory entry for accuracy. This does not imply endorsement or partnership.

### Verified partner

Used only after written permission.

Required evidence:

- email from official domain, or
- signed letter, or
- formal agreement, or
- public statement from organization.

### Advisor

Used only when a named person agrees to be listed as an advisor.

### Anonymous advisor

Used only with explicit permission and internal records.

---

## Donation links

At MVP stage, prefer linking only to official donation pages.

Do not process donations directly without:

- legal review;
- financial transparency plan;
- fraud prevention;
- sanctions screening where relevant;
- accounting process;
- clear terms.

Donation pages must say:

> Donations go directly to the listed organization through its official channel. Accountability Atlas does not handle these funds.

unless the project later creates a legally reviewed funding mechanism.

---

## Partner badge requirements

A verified partner badge should show:

- partner name;
- date verified;
- scope of relationship;
- confirmation type;
- public/redacted confirmation if permitted;
- contact for correction.

---

## Outreach ethics

Do not pressure organizations to support the project.

Do not use crisis language to guilt people into partnership.

Do not imply that silence equals approval.

---

## Organization safety

Some organizations may face political, legal, or operational risks. Respect requests to:

- avoid public listing;
- change wording;
- remove contact details;
- avoid showing logos;
- avoid implying relationship.

---

## Removal requests

If an organization asks to be removed or corrected, review promptly and respectfully.

If a listing uses only public information, maintainers may decide whether to keep it, but should prioritize safety and accuracy.
