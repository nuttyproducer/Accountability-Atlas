# Moderation Policy

## Purpose

Moderation protects accuracy, safety, dignity, and credibility.

---

## What moderation covers

Moderation may apply to:

- GitHub issues;
- pull requests;
- discussions;
- documentation;
- public comments if added;
- submitted corrections;
- research contributions;
- future evidence submissions;
- future organization submissions.

---

## Moderation principles

1. Safety first.
2. Accuracy over speed.
3. No hate or dehumanization.
4. No doxing.
5. No incitement.
6. No unverified claims as fact.
7. Respect affected communities.
8. Preserve correction history where appropriate.
9. Avoid public fights.
10. Keep sensitive material private.

---

## Prohibited content

Remove or reject content that includes:

- hate speech;
- threats;
- calls for violence;
- private personal information;
- harassment targets;
- revenge language;
- explicit instructions for illegal activity;
- graphic content without clear documentation need;
- misinformation;
- impersonation;
- spam;
- malicious links;
- malware;
- irrelevant political propaganda;
- false claims of partnership.

---

## Handling graphic content

Graphic content should not be posted in GitHub issues or public discussions.

If graphic content is necessary for future evidence workflows, it must be handled through reviewed systems with warnings, access limits, and safety review.

---

## Handling legal claims

Moderators should flag for review any content that:

- names a person as guilty;
- claims a legal finding without source;
- misstates court status;
- calls for prosecution without evidence;
- conflates allegations with judgments.

---

## Handling organization entries

Reject entries that:

- falsely claim partnership;
- include private contact details;
- lack source links;
- solicit funds through unofficial channels;
- appear fraudulent.

---

## Enforcement actions

Possible actions:

- ask for revision;
- edit wording;
- add warning label;
- close issue;
- reject pull request;
- remove content;
- temporarily restrict contributor;
- permanently ban contributor;
- escalate security issue privately.

---

## Moderator wellbeing

Moderation of atrocity-related content can be emotionally heavy. Moderators should:

- rotate duties;
- avoid repeated exposure to graphic material;
- use content warnings;
- take breaks;
- escalate difficult decisions;
- not moderate alone when high-risk.

---

## Appeals

If content is removed, contributors may ask for reconsideration calmly. Maintainers may uphold, revise, or reverse moderation decisions.

Harassment over moderation decisions is not allowed.
