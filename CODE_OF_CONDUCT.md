# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in Accountability Atlas a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, sexual identity and orientation, or political viewpoint, so long as participation follows the project mission and safety standards.

We are committed to a community rooted in human dignity, civilian protection, lawful accountability, and evidence-based work.

---

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- using welcoming and inclusive language;
- respecting different levels of experience;
- giving constructive feedback;
- focusing criticism on ideas and work, not people;
- admitting mistakes and correcting them;
- prioritizing safety, privacy, and accuracy;
- using calm language around sensitive topics;
- showing empathy toward affected civilians and contributors.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- harassment, intimidation, threats, or personal attacks;
- antisemitism, Islamophobia, racism, xenophobia, ethnic hatred, or dehumanization;
- calls for violence or revenge;
- doxing or sharing private personal information;
- targeting private individuals for harassment;
- publishing unverified claims as fact;
- glorifying suffering or using graphic content irresponsibly;
- sexualized language or imagery where not relevant;
- trolling, brigading, or sustained disruption;
- using project spaces to promote unrelated political agendas;
- attempting to pressure contributors into unsafe public statements.

---

## Special rule for this project

Because this project deals with atrocity documentation, legal accountability, and humanitarian crisis, contributors must avoid language or behavior that could:

- endanger witnesses;
- expose victims or families;
- incite harassment;
- weaken legal credibility;
- create collective blame against civilians or identity groups;
- turn the platform into a revenge or propaganda tool.

---

## Enforcement responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, pull requests, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers may temporarily or permanently ban any contributor for behavior that they consider inappropriate, threatening, offensive, harmful, or unsafe.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including:

- GitHub issues and pull requests;
- discussions;
- documentation;
- chat channels;
- social media when representing the project;
- events, calls, and meetings;
- direct messages related to project work.

---

## Reporting

Report conduct issues to the maintainer team using the private contact method listed in the repository once available.

Do not report sensitive conduct issues in public GitHub issues.

Reports should include:

- what happened;
- where it happened;
- when it happened;
- links or screenshots if available;
- whether immediate action is needed.

Maintainers will treat reports respectfully and with appropriate confidentiality.

---

## Enforcement guidelines

Maintainers may take actions including:

1. private clarification;
2. written warning;
3. removal of content;
4. temporary restriction from project spaces;
5. permanent ban;
6. escalation to platform administrators or legal authorities if threats or illegal activity occur.

---

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is adapted from common open-source community standards and the Contributor Covenant model, with additional safety rules for human-rights and atrocity-documentation work.
